2.2. Applicable Legal Framework

The prohibitions established in this section are supported by the following laws and international instruments:

Venezuelan Law:

  • Ley para la Protección de la Fauna Doméstica, Libre y en Cautiverio (Gaceta Oficial N.° 39.338, January 4, 2010): prohibits animal cruelty, abuse, abandonment, and mistreatment. Establishes penalties of community service, fines, and imprisonment of 1 to 4 years for acts of cruelty. Prohibits animal fighting and the use of animals for entertainment that causes suffering.
  • Ley Penal del Ambiente (Gaceta Oficial N.° 39.913, April 2, 2012): classifies crimes against fauna, including illegal trafficking, hunting, and commercialization of protected species. Penalties range from fines to imprisonment of up to 6 years depending on the offense and whether protected species are involved.
  • Ley de Protección a la Fauna Silvestre (1970, reformed): regulates the protection and conservation of wild fauna, prohibiting unauthorized capture, trade, and possession of wild and exotic species.
  • Ley Orgánica del Ambiente (2006): establishes the general framework for environmental and biodiversity protection, including fauna, as a matter of public and national interest.
  • Código Penal de Venezuela: Articles related to animal cruelty and property crimes applicable to cases of theft, fraud, or illegal appropriation of animals.

International Framework:

  • CITES (Convention on International Trade in Endangered Species of Wild Fauna and Flora, 1973): international treaty ratified by Venezuela and over 180 countries that regulates and prohibits the international commercial trade of endangered species. Violations carry criminal penalties in signatory countries.
  • Universal Declaration of Animal Rights (UNESCO, 1978): establishes that all animals have the right to live free from cruelty, exploitation, and unnecessary suffering.
  • OIE Terrestrial Animal Health Code (World Organisation for Animal Health): international standards for animal welfare, health, and humane treatment that member countries are expected to implement.
  • EU Regulation 1/2005 (European Union): regulates the protection of animals during transport and trade, applicable to users operating from EU jurisdictions.
  • Animal Welfare Act (United States, 1966): federal law regulating the treatment, sale, and transport of animals, applicable to users operating from U.S. jurisdictions.
  • Regional animal protection laws: users are additionally subject to all applicable animal protection laws in their country, state, province, or municipality of residence.
